Quick overview

Cars — short for "automobiles" — are wheeled motor vehicles used for transporting people. They evolved from early steam and electric prototypes to modern internal combustion and electric powertrains.

Key milestones

  • Late 19th century: first petrol-powered cars (Benz, Daimler).
  • Early 20th century: assembly line mass production (Ford Model T).
  • Late 20th — 21st century: electronics, safety systems, and EV revolution.

Common car categories

  • Hatchback — compact, practical.
  • Sedan — comfortable 4-door passenger cars.
  • SUV/Crossover — taller ride, more cargo room.
  • Pickup — cargo bed and towing capability.
  • Sports cars — performance-focused.
  • EVs (Electric Vehicles) — battery powered, lower emissions.

How cars work — simplified

At a high level a car needs three things: power source, transmission to deliver power, and wheels to move the vehicle. Below are simplified diagrams and quick notes.

SubsystemRole
Engine / MotorProduces rotational power (torque)
TransmissionMatches engine speed to wheel speed
Chassis & SuspensionSupports vehicle and smooths ride
BrakesSlow / stop the car
ElectricalControls, infotainment, safety systems

Tips for buyers

  • Define needs: passengers, cargo, city vs highway, towing.
  • Compare total cost of ownership (fuel, maintenance, insurance).
  • Test drive multiple models — evaluate visibility, comfort.
  • Check safety ratings and warranty coverage.
  • For used cars: verify service history and get a pre-purchase inspection.
